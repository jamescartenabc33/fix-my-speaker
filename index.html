FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the Fix My Speaker tool and how it works.

Is this tool safe for my phone?
Yes, completely. The sound frequencies used are within the normal operational range for smartphone speakers. The tool does not exceed the volume or frequency thresholds your speaker was engineered to handle. It is the safest way to clean a speaker without tools.
Does the Fix My Speaker tool work on iPhones?
Absolutely. Fix My Speaker works on all iPhone models, including those with IP67/IP68 water resistance ratings. Even resistant phones benefit from clearing residual moisture that makes its way into the speaker grille.
How long should I run the cleaning cycle?
For light water exposure (splash, brief rain), one 30–60 second cycle is typically enough. For heavier exposure — like dropping your phone in water — run 2–3 cycles with a short break and a gentle wipe of the grille between each cycle.
My speaker still sounds muffled after using the tool. What now?
First, make sure your volume is at maximum and run 2–3 more cycles. If the issue persists, internal water damage may have reached components beyond the speaker grille, and professional service may be the right next step. The tool is highly effective for grille-level blockage but cannot repair internal hardware damage.
Does this phone speaker cleaning tool work for the earpiece too?
Yes! Switch your phone's audio output to the earpiece (top speaker) in your settings, then run the same cleaning process. This is effective for muffled call audio, which is a common complaint after water exposure.
Does it work for Bluetooth speakers?
The tool is primarily optimized for built-in phone and tablet speakers. For external Bluetooth speakers, results may vary depending on the speaker housing design and grille configuration. It's worth trying, but results are not guaranteed.
Is Fix My Speaker really free?
100% free. No registration, no subscription, no in-app purchases, and no hidden fees. The tool runs entirely in your browser with no data collected. Open it, use it, close it — that's all.
